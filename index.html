Abstract


Vector quantization (VQ) is a method for deterministically learning features through discrete codebook representations. Recent works have utilized a visual tokenizer to discretize visual regions for self-supervised representation learning. However, a notable limitation of such tokenizers arises from their lack of semantics, as they are solely derived from the pre-task of reconstructing raw image pixels in an auto-encoder fashion. Additionally, issues like imbalanced codebook distribution and codebook collapse can adversely impact performance due to inefficient codebook utilization.

To address these challenges, we propose an innovative approach to semantic online clustering within the realm of image tokenization. Our methodology harnesses contrastive learning to enrich token semantics, leading to the establishment of a temporospatially consistent semantic codebook. Moreover, we introduce a pyramid feature learning pipeline that seamlessly integrates features at different abstraction levels, our technique adeptly captures image details and semantics concurrently, thereby fortifying reconstruction performance. Consequently, our approach significantly improves both reconstruction quality and performance across various tasks reliant on Vector Quantization (VQ) with an enhanced semantic codebook. Extensive validation across diverse datasets and tasks underscores the efficacy of our approach within the field of image tokenization.
